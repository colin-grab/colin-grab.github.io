Project

My recent, personal research project was inspired by ideas I had during my two master theses. I invested much of my spare time to write a research paper about an algorihtm I developed for financial market valuation.
This comprised all the various steps from ideation, analytical mathematics, full implementation of the algorithms in Python, testing on large scale real world financial data by extensive data analysis and hours of rewriting, generating graphics and analysis.

Below I will discuss some of the corner stones of the research and present in a simple way the most important concepts. For details of the math, derivation and extensive discussion of the methods consult the research paper.

Abstract

The introduced method serves to estimate distributions over time weighted sums of future price changes by combining ideas from predictive knowledge with concepts from reinforcement learning.

This is done in parallel for multiple factors of time and a set of fixed investment strategies. These strategies can be single assets or any combination thereof and are predefined as a model choice.

The methods can be used on a stand-alone basis or can easily be applied as extensions to an existing trading framework.

There are no behaviours learned or policies optimized in the sense of reinforcement learning.

What's it about?

Market Valuation

A statistical model with minimal assumptions that enables conditional valuation of single assets and portfolios, both in expected values as well as full estimated distributions. The method allows for simple integration of friction (e.g. transacion costs, market slippage) and can be applied to any strategy of choice.

Informative Features

Improved feature generation by utilizing intuitive and simple recursive estimation methods in combination with ideas from predictive knowledge.

Enhanced Learning Algorithms

The model can be used as extension to exisisting algorithms as additional losses to enhance the learning process as well as potentially lead to better performance of learned strategies or estimations (by supporting their feature creation & extraction of relevant information, increased learning speed).

Keeping it simple

Minimal Assumptions

The method relies on intuitive concepts of Bellman Equations and uses contraction to estimate distributions over returns. Simplified, this only requires the assumption of conditional stationarity of the process. In contrast to other methods in Finance no assumption on distributional forms or environment dynamics are needed.

Model-Free

No explicit model of the market and price dynamics need to be specified and the algorithms rely on model-free recursive estimation techniques. Nevertheless, the model frameworks are easily extendable to include model based approaches.

Intuitive Concepts

The method relies on intuitive concepts and can be applied to any strategy of choice. The similarity of reinforcement learning returns to discounted cashflows should be intuitive to anybody with a financial background.

What happens from now on is equal to
what happens tomorrow + what happens after tomorrow

Basic Theory

Intuition

Above statement is the basis on which many recursive algorithms are built on. While this simple statement obviously always holds, to exploit it, some restrictions are required. To be able to use the equality for estimation, there needs to be consistency in what is going to happen. Specifically, there need be rules about how decisions are made and measures (e.g a number) to quantify the value of what happens.

Value Functions

Value functions, simplified, are above intuition translated into mathematics. They are a function to quantify everything that happens from now on and are defined as the expected sum of what is going to happen in the future when making decisions based on some fixed rule and weigthed by some factor to take into acount the effects of time.

Estimation

By exploiting their recursive properties, the concept of value functions can be used to estimate the expected weighted sum of future rewards conditional on some state of the environment. The main focus in my research is a distributional version of value functions, which uses contraction to estimate not only expected values but full distributions over returns.

How is this new?

Different Focus

Predominately, existing reinforcement learning literature applied to finance & trading focus on learning optimal behaviour. While they sometimes incorporate value functions in the learning process, taking a step back and focussing on the value functions themselves as the quantity of interest and as the main driver is unique. Moreover, utilizing value functions in combination with predictive knowledge and time considerations for market valuation is new.

Estimate Distributions

Additionally, the extensions to incorporate a distributional variant in combination with predictive knowledge and time considerations is to the best of my knowledge a completely new way of approaching the topic and has not been publicly introduced to finance yet.

Finance Logic

Why do we care?

Consult the figure below, where the actual price of an asset and the realized returns for different time considerations are shown. The more weight is given to future rewards, the earlier the respective lines fall or rise before the price. For any given time, the values of the returns can not be observed and will only be known at some time in the future once sufficient future rewards are observed. Clearly, having good estimates of their distributions or expected values is highly valuable.

Reward Definition

The reward definition is a modeling choice and can incorporate both risk and utility considerations if needed, enabling the models to be tailored to preferences. For the current investigation, I considered the one step rewards as the logarithm of the one step change in a portfolio's value.

Customizable

My method can be customized to strategies and time considerations of interest, allowing tailoring the methods to specific needs and contexts.

Intuitive Indicator

If one had trustworthy estimates of the expected returns or distributions, one can easily construct intuitive indicators & signals from the estimates.

The figure shows the actual price of an asset in dark blue and realized returns for different factors of time consideration (x-axis: t for time). Clearly, the lines of the realized returns (especially visible for highest weighting of time in light blue and orange) drop & rise earlier than the actual price.
Thus, if one could have good estimates of these lines, one would have a competitive edge and could profit a lot.

Learning
is hard

Predictive
& General Knowledge

Most machine learning approaches need to learn a representation of the data while learning the solution to some main objective at the same time. This increases complexity and is a source of failure.
Predictive Knowledge relies on the idea that algorithms that are trained to solve multiple tasks on the same data are forced to learn more information dense representations of the world. Incorporating this intuition by estimating values for single assets and multiple different trading strategies at the same time provides more feedback during learning and enforces more robust features.

State
Representation

While the method does not solve the problem of choosing optimal or sufficient data to use as inputs, it supports the learning algorithms and enhances information extraction of the data, potentially leading to better results for the same data.

Replay Buffers
& Target Networks

Known methods from reinforcement learning to stabilize and robustify the training process to mitigate sources of complexity and instability can be directly included in the training process of the method. Mainly, these are the use of prioritized replay buffers that allow more frequent sampling of relevant training transitions to speed up learning as well as target networks to stabilize recursive estimation.

Evaluation
(is hard)

Evaluation of Distributions

The true distributions or expected values of the return can never be observed and only one realization of the return is observed for each time step. It is thus very difficult to evaluate the correctness of estimated distributions. See Section 4 of the paper for an extensive discussion thereof.

Code

What is needed?

The methods require implementation of
a) a model function, typically a neural network
b) some learning rule that can calculate losses, their gradients and apply them to the model function parameters for multiple strategies and time considerations in parallell (based on observed rewards)
c) an environment that can calculate one-step rewards for different strategies and provide the representations of market states.

View Code

An example implementation of the method in Python is available on my Github page. It includes learning algorithms with experience replay, target networks, environments, corresponding reward and strategy functions.

Data

Real World Market Data

For the quantitative studies performed, actual financial market data sets were exploited. The models were trained for three different asset classes using close prices of one minute intervals. The two asset classes stocks and ETFs comprise data from First Rate Data and close prices of crypto currencies were taken from Binance.

Constructed Inputs

Based on the raw market data, I constructed features based on open & close prices to serve as inputs to the training process. The choice of input data is a modelling choice and should be conducted using feasible statistical procedures or economic logic.

FAQ

If I already have variables and features, can I combine them?

Yes, absolutely. Existing features and variables can be used both as inputs to the method or in combination with the estimated distributions and expected values as input to trading algorithms. Further, adding additional losses based on my method to an existing learning algorithm with your current features can support the algorithm to extract information more efficiently from your features and potentially increase the performance of these algorithms.

Why not just use a supervised learning approach to estimate prices or returns?

The method herein has the advantage that it builds on well-establihed and researched mathematical foundations to estimate a quantity that is both unique and guaranteed to exist, if the minimal assumptions hold. This is in large contrast to a supervised learning method that either requires very restrictive assumptions and modelling or is a complete black-box approach based on the assumption (rather hope) that one can estimate prices, returns etc.
Additionally, the true conditional distributions are never observed and no supervised approach is therefore capable of estimating them.
Another distinction that needs to be pointed out is that the method herein estimates the distribution (or expected value) of a weighted sum of future log returns of strategies versus supposedly trying to predict single log returns.

How will this influence learning speed?

The use of multiple additional auxiliary losses introduces additional computations (e.g. losses, their gradients and applying them) during the learning process. However, when utilizing additional losses most algorithms reach better performance with fewer samples and exhibit more robust learning as well as better features, i.e. they receive more information to learn per sample.
